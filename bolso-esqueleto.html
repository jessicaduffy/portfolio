The Brief

A fashion industry
that creates waste
by design

The fast-fashion accessory market runs on disposability. Synthetic leather — often marketed as a sustainable alternative — has a lifespan of just 2–10 years and ranks among China's most polluting industries. Meanwhile, genuine leather goods crafted with care can last 50+ years but rarely offer the personalization today's consumer wants.

The opportunity is not to choose between quality and individuality — it's to design a system that offers both.

50+
years lifespan, natural leather

vs. 2–10 years for synthetic alternatives — a difference that reframes "sustainable" entirely.

The Design Response

Design a durable, modular object in upcycled leather that users can personalize, repair, and evolve — combining traditional leatherwork with digital fabrication tools.

Craft

Traditional Craft

The hand is still
the primary tool

Digital tools set the structure; hand skills give the object its soul. No laser cuts the final stitch. No printer closes the snap.

  • Upcycled leather jacket — bag body
  • Secondhand denim — modular inner linings
  • Saddle stitch and hand-set press studs
  • Machine sewing for structural seams

Digital Fabrication

Technology as a finishing
language, not a replacement

Each digital technique was chosen for what it uniquely enables — precision cuts the hand cannot achieve, and sculptural forms impossible to sew.

  • Laser cutting — precise organic apertures in leather
  • 3D modelling in Blender — handle form development
  • FDM 3D printing — chain-link handle structure
  • 3D pen welding — hardware integration
  • Gemini AI — concept visualisation and iteration

The System

One skeleton,
infinite combinations

The "skeleton" concept reframes the bag as a permanent chassis — the piece you keep for life — and the linings, handles, and chain as swappable expressions of mood, occasion, or season.

The Skeleton

Upcycled leather body with laser-cut apertures. Structural, permanent, and designed to age beautifully. The holes reveal the lining beneath, making the inner fabric a visible design element.

Interchangeable Linings

Two linings included — a denim version and a silk-texture version in chartreuse — attached by hand-set press studs. Swap them out as the lining wears or your mood changes.

Three Handles

A 3D-printed chain handle, a vintage shoulder strap, and an embellished rope handle — each changing the bag's silhouette from casual to editorial to evening.

A Draped Chain

A brass-toned chain worn across the body of the bag — functional hardware as jewellery. Optional, stackable, and easily replaced.

Reflection

"Technology doesn't replace the hand — it extends what the hand can say."

A new model for
conscious accessories

The Bolso Esqueleto demonstrates that sustainability and personalization are not opposing values — they are the same design brief approached from different angles. By separating the permanent (the skeleton) from the changeable (the linings and handles), the project proposes a repair-and-swap model that extends product life without sacrificing individual expression.

The process confirmed a key insight: laser cutting functioned as a surface detail, while 3D printing was genuinely generative — enabling a handle form that would be impossible through traditional leatherwork. The most interesting design territory exists precisely at that boundary.
